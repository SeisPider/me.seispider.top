Xiao Xiao

PhD candidate of Geophysics

University of Science and Technology of China

Xiao Xiao is a PhD candidate in Geophysics at University of Science and Technology of China, supervised by Prof. Lianxing Wen.

His research is directed toward understanding the Earth’s structure in the crust and upper mantle with seismological tools (e.g., seismic tomography and receiver function analysis).

Xiao Xiao, Shihua Cheng, Jianping Wu, Weilai Wang, Li Sun, Xiaoxin Wang, Lianxing Wen
May, 2021 Geophysical Journal International
Shallow seismic structure beneath China revealed by P wave polarization, Rayleigh wave ellipticity and receiver function

  1. We develope a new method to constrain shallow seismic structure based on P polarization, Rayleigh wave ellipticity and receiver function.
  2. We construct a high-resolution shallow 3D seismic model in the top 10 km of the upper crust in the continental Cina.
  3. Our 3D seismic model has a spatial resolution of $0.6^°-1.2^°$ in the north-south seismic belt and the trans-north China orogen, and $1^°-2^°$ in the rest of the continental China (except the Tarim basin and the southwest Tibet).
